# theory · PROV-O — the lineage model (how to describe WHERE a series came from and HOW it was produced)

> **Product line:** JPM-Markets re-engineering **data-analytics service** (Project 3 — re-engineering
> DataQuery + Fusion). **NOT Lumina.** Greenfield Python/FastAPI/data-engineering stack. This file is
> *theory* — the generic, reusable vocabulary. The build recipe that applies it lives in
> `patterns-lineage-capture.md` (the operational lineage-capture pipeline).
>
> **What this answers.** Every series we ingest needs a machine-readable stamp: *where did this number
> come from, who produced it, when, through what fetch-and-transform run, and (the load-bearing one for
> us) is it licensed for commercial display?* W3C **PROV-O** is the W3C-Recommendation vocabulary for the
> first four; our `commercialOk` gate rides on top. This doc is "how do we *describe* lineage" — the
> grammar — so the capture recipe has a fixed target to write into.
>
> **The one-sentence thesis.** *Borrow PROV-O's **names** (Entity / Activity / Agent / wasGeneratedBy /
> wasDerivedFrom / wasAttributedTo) as the canonical schema for our provenance stamp — and especially
> `wasDerivedFrom` as the **contamination edge** that makes "this GREEN composite secretly inherits a RED
> input" a queryable graph fact — without committing to ship an RDF triplestore at v1.* The model is the
> mandate; the triplestore is optional.
>
> **JPM-theory anchor.** The data-analytics `00-theory.md` lists, in its Tier-3 "provenance standards"
> finding, *"**W3C PROV** (entities/activities/agents) for the model + **OpenLineage** for run-level
> pipeline lineage"* and leaves "provenance depth" as **open-question #4** (*"is per-series static
> `commercialOk` + source + as-of enough for v1, or is full W3C-PROV/OpenLineage transform-lineage a hard
> requirement?"*). This doc is the substance behind that bullet and the framework for answering #4.
> ([`.agents/jpm-markets-reengineering/financial-data-analytics-service/00-theory.md`](../../../../.agents/jpm-markets-reengineering/financial-data-analytics-service/00-theory.md), lines 104, 120, 205.)

## 0. Why a *standard* vocabulary, and why this one

You could invent a provenance schema. `{"source": "yahoo", "fetched": "...", "licensed": false}` — done in
five minutes. The reason not to:

1. **Provenance is a graph, not a flat field.** A composite series (a yield-curve spread, an index, a
   sentiment composite) is *derived from* other series, each of which was *generated by* a fetch run,
   *attributed to* a provider, possibly itself *derived from* a third series. The interesting questions —
   *"does this GREEN number transitively depend on a RED input?"*, *"if treasury.gov revises the 10Y, which
   of my published composites are stale?"* — are **reachability queries over edges**. A flat field can't
   answer them; a graph can. PROV-O is a graph vocabulary with exactly the right edges already named.

2. **Someone already did the hard modelling, with adversarial review.** PROV-O is a 2013 **W3C
   Recommendation** ([w3.org/TR/prov-o](https://www.w3.org/TR/prov-o/)) — the OWL2 encoding of the PROV
   Data Model ([w3.org/TR/prov-dm](https://www.w3.org/TR/prov-dm/)), built by a working group over years
   and used across science, government open-data, and workflow systems. The FAIR community treats it as the
   reusability backbone: *"information about entities, activities, and people involved in producing a piece
   of data or thing, which can be used to form assessments about its quality, reliability or
   trustworthiness"* ([FAIR Cookbook, provenance recipe](https://faircookbook.elixir-europe.org/content/recipes/reusability/provenance.html)).
   Reinventing the vocabulary means re-deriving the same three classes and ~a dozen relations and getting
   the edge directions subtly wrong.

3. **It composes with the rest of the data-platform standard stack.** Our catalog model is **DCAT v3**
   ([w3.org/TR/vocab-dcat-3](https://www.w3.org/TR/vocab-dcat-3/)); DCAT explicitly reuses PROV
   (`prov:wasGeneratedBy`, `prov:wasDerivedFrom`, `prov:qualifiedAttribution`) for dataset provenance.
   Per-distribution schema is **Frictionless Table Schema**. Operational run capture is **OpenLineage**
   (§8). Picking PROV-O for the lineage layer means these four standards interlock instead of fighting.

> **The discipline this preserves.** "Borrow the names, not necessarily the triplestore." We model our
> provenance stamp on PROV-O's classes and properties so that (a) the *semantics* are battle-tested, (b)
> we can serialize to real PROV (RDF/JSON-LD/PROV-N) the day a consumer asks, and (c) the contamination
> rule becomes a graph edge — **without** the v1 obligation to stand up Apache Jena / a triplestore. We
> store the same graph in plain Postgres rows (see `patterns-lineage-capture.md`) and call it PROV-shaped.
> §7 is the full argument for why this is the right scale call.

## 1. The three core classes — Entity, Activity, Agent

PROV's entire model rests on three classes and the relations between them. Namespace
`http://www.w3.org/ns/prov#`, conventional prefix `prov:`. ([w3.org/TR/prov-o](https://www.w3.org/TR/prov-o/).)

| Class | PROV-O IRI | W3C definition (verbatim) | In OUR system |
|---|---|---|---|
| **prov:Entity** | `prov#Entity` | *"a physical, digital, conceptual, or other kind of thing with some fixed aspects"* | a **time-series**, a **Distribution** (the CSV/Parquet file), a **catalog dataset**, a single **observation snapshot** |
| **prov:Activity** | `prov#Activity` | *"something that occurs over a period of time and acts upon or with entities"* | one **fetch-normalize-store run** (an ingest job execution); a **transform** that builds a composite |
| **prov:Agent** | `prov#Agent` | *"something that bears some form of responsibility for an activity taking place, for the existence of an entity, or for another agent's activity"* | the **provider org** (treasury.gov, Yahoo, FRED); **our ingest pipeline** (a `prov:SoftwareAgent`); the **operator** who configured it |

The PROV primer's plain-language framing is worth keeping in mind because it disciplines what goes where
([w3.org/TR/prov-primer](https://www.w3.org/TR/prov-primer/)):

- **Entity** — *"physical, digital, conceptual, or other kinds of thing"* whose provenance we describe.
  Nouns with **fixed aspects**. The 10Y yield series *as of the 2026-06-24 fetch* is an entity; if a later
  fetch revises it, that is a **new entity** (`wasRevisionOf` the first) — entities don't mutate, you make
  a new one. This immutability is exactly the discipline a financial point-in-time store needs.
- **Activity** — *"how entities come into existence and how their attributes change to become new
  entities."* Verbs with **duration** (start time, end time). Our ingest run is an activity; it *uses*
  upstream-response entities and *generates* the normalized-series entity.
- **Agent** — *"takes a role in an activity such that the agent can be assigned some degree of
  responsibility."* The **who's-accountable** axis. A provider is the agent the series is *attributed to*;
  our pipeline is the agent the run is *associated with*.

### 1.1 Agent subtypes (expanded terms)

PROV-O ships three `prov:Agent` subclasses; use them — they carry meaning a license auditor cares about
([w3.org/TR/prov-o](https://www.w3.org/TR/prov-o/)):

| Subclass | IRI | Use for |
|---|---|---|
| **prov:Organization** | `prov#Organization` | the **provider** — U.S. Treasury, Federal Reserve Board, FRED/St-Louis-Fed, Yahoo, CoinGecko. *The license attaches to this org's fetch path.* |
| **prov:SoftwareAgent** | `prov#SoftwareAgent` | **our ingest pipeline** — the code that ran the fetch (carry a version/sha so a bad transform is traceable to a release). |
| **prov:Person** | `prov#Person` | the **operator** who configured/approved a source (rarely needed per-series; useful in the audit trail). |

> **Why the Organization split matters for us specifically.** Our central licensing rule — *"the license
> attaches to the FETCH PATH, not the concept"* ([`commercial-ok-gate.md`](../../../../.claude/rules/commercial-ok-gate.md))
> — is, in PROV terms, *"`commercialOk` is a property of the `prov:wasAttributedTo` edge to the provider
> `prov:Organization`, not of the `prov:Entity` (the number) itself."* The 10Y yield entity is the same
> number; attributed-to `treasury.gov` it's GREEN, attributed-to `yahoo` it's RED. The provenance graph
> makes that legible because the agent is a first-class node, not a string buried in the value.

## 2. The load-bearing properties (edge directions matter)

This is the part to get exactly right, because **the direction of every edge is fixed by the spec** and
reversing one inverts the meaning. All definitions and domain/range below are verbatim from
[w3.org/TR/prov-o §3 (starting-point terms)](https://www.w3.org/TR/prov-o/).

| Property | Domain → Range | Reads as | Definition (verbatim) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **prov:wasGeneratedBy** | Entity → Activity | *entity was produced by activity* | *"completion of production of a new entity by an activity"* |
| **prov:used** | Activity → Entity | *activity consumed entity* | *"beginning of utilizing an entity by an activity"* |
| **prov:wasInformedBy** | Activity → Activity | *activity B got an entity from activity A* | *"exchange of an entity by two activities"* |
| **prov:wasDerivedFrom** | Entity → Entity | *entity B came from entity A* | *"transformation of an entity into another"* |
| **prov:wasAttributedTo** | Entity → Agent | *entity is credited to agent* | *"ascribing of an entity to an agent"* |
| **prov:wasAssociatedWith** | Activity → Agent | *agent ran / is responsible for activity* | *"assignment of responsibility to an agent for an activity"* |
| **prov:actedOnBehalfOf** | Agent → Agent | *agent acted for a delegating agent* | *"assignment of authority and responsibility to an agent (by itself or by another agent)"* |
| **prov:generatedAtTime** | Entity → xsd:dateTime | *when the entity came into being* | the instant of generation |
| **prov:startedAtTime** | Activity → xsd:dateTime | *when the run began* | start instant |
| **prov:endedAtTime** | Activity → xsd:dateTime | *when the run finished* | end instant |
| **prov:invalidatedAtTime** | Entity → xsd:dateTime | *when the entity stopped being valid* | invalidation instant |

### 2.1 The two halves of one fact

Notice `wasGeneratedBy` (Entity→Activity) and `used` (Activity→Entity) are **opposite-direction siblings**
describing the same run from the two ends:

```
   upstream_response  --used-->  fetchRun  <--wasGeneratedBy--  normalized_series
   (input entity)                (activity)                     (output entity)
```

A complete ingest fact is: *the `fetchRun` activity `used` the `upstream_response` entity and the
`normalized_series` entity `wasGeneratedBy` the `fetchRun`.* You assert both edges; together they say
"this output came from that input via this run."

### 2.2 `wasDerivedFrom` — the contamination edge (the one that earns its keep for us)

`prov:wasDerivedFrom` (Entity → Entity) is *"transformation of an entity into another."* It is the edge
that makes **lineage transitive**, and for this product line it is the single most valuable PROV property,
because it is the graph form of our **contamination rule**.

> **The contamination rule** (from [`commercial-ok-gate.md`](../../../../.claude/rules/commercial-ok-gate.md),
> as surfaced in `red-team-negation-loop.md` goal F2): *"a composite that inherits a RED input yet claims
> GREEN"* is a CRITICAL licensing violation. A spread = `treasury_10y (GREEN) − some_corporate_yield`. If
> the corporate yield came from a RED fetch path, the spread is **RED**, no matter that one leg is
> public-domain. License does not dilute; it contaminates upward.

In PROV terms that rule is a **reachability query over `wasDerivedFrom` edges**:

```
:spread_ig_10y
   a prov:Entity ;
   prov:wasDerivedFrom :treasury_10y ;       # GREEN leg
   prov:wasDerivedFrom :corp_ig_yield ;      # RED leg  <-- contaminates
   prov:wasGeneratedBy :composite_build_run .

# the licensing verdict for :spread_ig_10y is:
#   GREEN  iff  every entity reachable via wasDerivedFrom* is commercialOk:true
#   RED    iff  ANY ancestor is commercialOk:false
```

Without the derivation edge, you cannot mechanically prove a composite is clean — you'd be eyeballing it.
With it, "is this series safe to display commercially?" becomes a graph traversal you can run in CI (the
`/sources-lint`-class audit, extended to composites). **This is the concrete reason to adopt PROV's model
rather than a flat `source` string: the flat string can't express "derived from," so it can't catch
contamination.** The capture recipe (`patterns-lineage-capture.md`) stores these edges as rows in a
`lineage_edge` table; this doc establishes that the edge to store is named `wasDerivedFrom` and points
**child → parent** (newer → older).

#### Derivation subtypes — three refinements of `wasDerivedFrom`

PROV-DM defines three sub-properties of `wasDerivedFrom`, each a more specific claim about *how* the
derivation happened ([w3.org/TR/prov-dm §2.1.2](https://www.w3.org/TR/prov-dm/);
[w3.org/TR/prov-o](https://www.w3.org/TR/prov-o/)). All three still contaminate, and all three are useful
financial lineage:

| Sub-property | Domain → Range | Definition (verbatim, PROV-DM) | Our use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **prov:wasRevisionOf** | Entity → Entity | *"the derived Entity contains substantial content from the original Entity"* (e.g. two editions of a book) | **a re-fetch that corrects/revises a series** — treasury.gov revises last week's 10Y; new series `wasRevisionOf` old. This is how a point-in-time store records "as-of" history. |
| **prov:hadPrimarySource** | Entity → Entity | *"cites a preceding Entity produced by some agent with direct experience and knowledge about the topic"* (a sensor reading, a journal) | **the original publisher behind an aggregator** — FRED republishes a Treasury series; FRED's copy `hadPrimarySource` the Treasury original. *Critically distinguishes the licensable fetch path from the upstream concept.* |
| **prov:wasQuotedFrom** | Entity → Entity | *"a new Entity created by repeating some or all of [a larger original]"* | **a slice/excerpt of a larger dataset** — your "USD IG spreads since 2010" series quoted from a vendor's full credit universe. |

Each has a **qualified form** (§4) — `prov:Revision`, `prov:PrimarySource`, `prov:Quotation` — when you
need to annotate the derivation itself (e.g. *which agent* performed the revision).

### 2.3 `wasInformedBy` — activity-to-activity hand-off

`prov:wasInformedBy` (Activity → Activity) records that one activity received an entity produced by
another, *without naming the intermediate entity.* In a pipeline:

```
fetchRun  <--wasInformedBy--  normalizeRun  <--wasInformedBy--  composeRun
```

Use it for the **coarse pipeline DAG** ("the compose step ran after, and depended on, the normalize step")
when you don't want to thread every intermediate entity. It's the PROV analogue of OpenLineage's
job-to-job edges (§8). For us it's optional at v1 — the entity-level `used`/`wasGeneratedBy`/`wasDerivedFrom`
edges already carry the load — but it's the right tool when you model multi-stage transforms.

### 2.4 `actedOnBehalfOf` — delegation chains

`prov:actedOnBehalfOf` (Agent → Agent) records delegation: agent A acted on behalf of agent B. The primer's
example: a journalist `actedOnBehalfOf` the newspaper org. For us: *our ingest `SoftwareAgent`
`actedOnBehalfOf` our platform org;* or *a vendor's redistribution arm `actedOnBehalfOf` the original
exchange.* Mostly an audit nicety; it does **not** alter the licensing verdict (that rides on
`wasAttributedTo`), so don't overweight it.

## 3. How a per-series provenance record decomposes into PROV terms

This is the crux of the doc — the **mapping** from the flat fields a fetcher actually has to the PROV graph.
Given a normalized series and the metadata our TET fetcher already produces (see the `data-normalization-tet`
skill), every field has a PROV home:

| Our per-series field | PROV term | Edge / typing |
|---|---|---|
| the series itself | `prov:Entity` | the output node |
| the Distribution file (Parquet/CSV) | `prov:Entity` (`prov:specializationOf` the series, or a `dcat:Distribution`) | a second entity for the materialized bytes |
| **`source`** (which provider) | `prov:Agent` (`prov:Organization`) | `series prov:wasAttributedTo provider` |
| **`fetchPath`** (the URL/endpoint hit) | a property of the **Activity** (the run that hit it) + the basis of the license verdict | `run prov:used <upstream_response>`; the verdict attaches here |
| **`fetchedAt`** (when we pulled it) | `prov:generatedAtTime` on the entity *and/or* `prov:endedAtTime` on the run | timestamp |
| **the fetch+normalize run** | `prov:Activity` | `series prov:wasGeneratedBy run` |
| **our pipeline + version/sha** | `prov:Agent` (`prov:SoftwareAgent`) | `run prov:wasAssociatedWith pipeline` |
| **the transform** (alias map, unit scaling, tz→UTC) | the same `prov:Activity`, optionally a `prov:Plan` (§4) | encoded as the activity's plan/role |
| **upstream/input series** (for composites) | `prov:Entity` per input | `series prov:wasDerivedFrom input` (the contamination edges) |
| **`commercialOk`** | **a qualified `prov:Attribution`** carrying the license (§5) | rides on the `wasAttributedTo` edge |
| **`asOf` / revision** | `prov:wasRevisionOf` to the prior version | derivation subtype |

### 3.1 The canonical worked example (one ingest, fully PROV-stamped)

Take a concrete fetch: *we pull the U.S. 10-year Treasury constant-maturity yield from treasury.gov on
2026-06-24, normalize it, and store it.* Here is the complete PROV graph in **Turtle** — the same shape the
PROV primer's chart example uses ([w3.org/TR/prov-primer](https://www.w3.org/TR/prov-primer/);
[FAIR Cookbook](https://faircookbook.elixir-europe.org/content/recipes/reusability/provenance.html)),
re-grounded in our domain:

```turtle
@prefix prov: <http://www.w3.org/ns/prov#> .
@prefix xsd:  <http://www.w3.org/2001/XMLSchema#> .
@prefix dct:  <http://purl.org/dc/terms/> .
@prefix lic:  <https://example.platform/ns/license#> .   # OUR licensing namespace
@prefix :     <https://example.platform/series/> .

# ---- the OUTPUT entity: the normalized series we will serve ----
:treasury_10y_2026-06-24
    a prov:Entity ;
    dct:title "US 10Y Treasury constant-maturity yield" ;
    prov:wasGeneratedBy   :ingestRun_8f3c ;          # produced by the run
    prov:wasAttributedTo   :treasuryGov ;            # credited to the provider org
    prov:generatedAtTime  "2026-06-24T06:10:00Z"^^xsd:dateTime ;
    # the license verdict, qualified onto the attribution (see §5):
    prov:qualifiedAttribution [
        a prov:Attribution ;
        prov:agent      :treasuryGov ;
        lic:commercialOk true ;                      # GREEN: public-domain fetch path
        lic:basis       "17 USC §105 (US-gov public domain)" ;
    ] .

# ---- the INPUT entity: the raw upstream response we used ----
:treasuryGov_response_8f3c
    a prov:Entity ;
    dct:title "treasury.gov daily-treasury-rates XML, 2026-06-24" .

# ---- the ACTIVITY: our fetch+normalize+store run ----
:ingestRun_8f3c
    a prov:Activity ;
    prov:used              :treasuryGov_response_8f3c ;   # consumed the raw response
    prov:wasAssociatedWith :ingestPipeline_v1_4_2 ;       # run by our pipeline agent
    prov:startedAtTime     "2026-06-24T06:09:58Z"^^xsd:dateTime ;
    prov:endedAtTime       "2026-06-24T06:10:00Z"^^xsd:dateTime ;
    # the transform recipe, as a Plan (see §4):
    prov:qualifiedAssociation [
        a prov:Association ;
        prov:agent   :ingestPipeline_v1_4_2 ;
        prov:hadPlan :treasuryGov_10y_transform ;         # alias map + unit + tz rules
    ] .

# ---- the AGENTS ----
:treasuryGov
    a prov:Organization, prov:Agent ;
    dct:title "U.S. Department of the Treasury" .

:ingestPipeline_v1_4_2
    a prov:SoftwareAgent, prov:Agent ;
    dct:title "platform ingest pipeline" ;
    dct:hasVersion "1.4.2+sha.a1b2c3d" .

# ---- the PLAN: the deterministic transform applied ----
:treasuryGov_10y_transform
    a prov:Plan ;
    dct:description "rename BC_10YEAR->yield; value already percent; date tz UTC" .
```

Read top-to-bottom this says: *the series was generated by run `8f3c`, attributed to Treasury (which makes
it GREEN/public-domain), generated at 06:10; the run used the raw treasury.gov XML, was run by pipeline
v1.4.2 following a named transform plan, between 06:09:58 and 06:10:00.* Every field our fetcher had now
has a typed, queryable home — and the `commercialOk` verdict is bolted to the **attribution edge to the
provider org**, exactly where the fetch-path-not-concept rule says it belongs.

### 3.2 The composite case (where contamination shows up)

```turtle
:spread_ig_10y_2026-06-24
    a prov:Entity ;
    dct:title "IG corporate − 10Y Treasury spread" ;
    prov:wasGeneratedBy :composeRun_d1e2 ;
    prov:wasDerivedFrom :treasury_10y_2026-06-24 ;     # GREEN leg
    prov:wasDerivedFrom :corp_ig_yield_2026-06-24 ;    # RED leg (vendor free tier)
    prov:qualifiedAttribution [
        a prov:Attribution ;
        prov:agent      :ourPlatform ;
        lic:commercialOk false ;       # contaminated: ANY RED ancestor -> RED
        lic:basis       "derived: inherits RED from corp_ig_yield fetch path"
    ] .
```

The verdict `false` here is not a human guess — it is **derivable** by walking `wasDerivedFrom` from the
spread and `OR`-ing the ancestors' `commercialOk`. That mechanizability is the whole point.

## 4. The qualified pattern — annotating the *relationship*, not just the nodes

Sometimes the fact you need to record isn't about an entity or an activity but about the **edge between
them**. "Derek used the dataset *in the role of* analyst input." "This entity is attributed to the provider
*under license X*." The plain binary properties (`used`, `wasAttributedTo`) can't hang an attribute on the
relationship. PROV solves this with the **qualified pattern**: reify the influence into an intermediate
object you can decorate. ([w3.org/TR/prov-o §3.2](https://www.w3.org/TR/prov-o/);
[primer](https://www.w3.org/TR/prov-primer/).)

### 4.1 The mechanism

Every starting-point property has a **qualified twin** that points at a reified *Influence* class, which in
turn names the influencer plus any extra attributes (role, time, plan, …):

| Plain property | Qualifying property | Reified class | The influencer property inside it |
|---|---|---|---|
| `prov:wasGeneratedBy` | `prov:qualifiedGeneration` | `prov:Generation` | `prov:activity` |
| `prov:used` | `prov:qualifiedUsage` | `prov:Usage` | `prov:entity` |
| `prov:wasDerivedFrom` | `prov:qualifiedDerivation` | `prov:Derivation` | `prov:entity` |
| `prov:wasAttributedTo` | `prov:qualifiedAttribution` | `prov:Attribution` | `prov:agent` |
| `prov:wasAssociatedWith` | `prov:qualifiedAssociation` | `prov:Association` | `prov:agent` |
| `prov:actedOnBehalfOf` | `prov:qualifiedDelegation` | `prov:Delegation` | `prov:agent` |
| `prov:wasInformedBy` | `prov:qualifiedCommunication` | `prov:Communication` | `prov:activity` |

All the reified classes are subclasses of `prov:Influence` (`prov:EntityInfluence` / `prov:ActivityInfluence`
/ `prov:AgentInfluence`). The annotation properties you hang on them:

- **`prov:hadRole`** (→ `prov:Role`) — the function the entity/agent served (e.g. "data-to-compose",
  "analyst", "license-grantor").
- **`prov:atTime`** (→ xsd:dateTime) — the instant the influence occurred (e.g. the precise generation
  time, finer than the run's end time).
- **`prov:hadPlan`** (→ `prov:Plan`) — on an `Association`, the recipe/steps the agent followed.
- **`prov:hadActivity`** (→ `prov:Activity`) — on a `Derivation`, *which* activity performed the transform.

### 4.2 The primer's worked qualified examples (verbatim shape)

Plain usage with a role becomes ([primer](https://www.w3.org/TR/prov-primer/)):

```turtle
exc:compose1 prov:qualifiedUsage [
    a prov:Usage ;
    prov:entity  exg:dataset1 ;
    prov:hadRole exc:dataToCompose
] .
```

Plain association with a role:

```turtle
exc:compose1 prov:qualifiedAssociation [
    a prov:Association ;
    prov:agent   exc:derek ;
    prov:hadRole exc:analyst
] .
```

Qualified generation with a precise time:

```turtle
:bar_chart prov:qualifiedGeneration [
    a prov:Generation ;
    prov:activity :illustrationActivity ;
    prov:atTime   "2011-07-14T15:52:14Z"^^xsd:dateTime ;
] .
```

> **Rule of thumb.** Assert the **plain** binary property *always* (it's what queries and reasoners
> expect); add the **qualified** form *only when you have something to say about the edge.* The qualified
> form does not replace the plain one — best practice is to assert both. (PROV-O's `prov:influencer`
> machinery even infers the plain edge from the qualified one, but don't rely on a reasoner being present.)

### 4.3 Why WE need exactly one qualified form: Attribution-carries-license

This is the practical payoff for our system. The `commercialOk` verdict is **a property of the
attribution edge** (entity ↔ provider-agent), not of the entity. So we attach it to `prov:Attribution`:

```turtle
:treasury_10y_2026-06-24
    prov:wasAttributedTo :treasuryGov ;            # plain edge — always assert
    prov:qualifiedAttribution [                    # qualified — carries the license
        a prov:Attribution ;
        prov:agent       :treasuryGov ;
        prov:hadRole     lic:dataPublisher ;
        lic:commercialOk true ;
        lic:basis        "17 USC §105" ;
        lic:ledgerRow    "sources-ledger#treasury-gov-rates" ;   # link to the GREEN row
        dct:license      <https://www.usa.gov/government-works>
    ] .
```

This is the cleanest place in the whole model to bind license to fetch-path: the **agent** is the provider,
the **edge** is the attribution, and the license is a fact *about that edge*. A different provider for the
same number is a different `wasAttributedTo` edge with a different `Attribution` carrying a different
verdict — which is precisely the "same number, different license, depending on where you fetched it"
semantics the gate demands. (Cross-link: the ledger row referenced is in
[`.claude/memory/sources-ledger.md`](../../../../.claude/memory/sources-ledger.md) for the Lumina repo; the
data-analytics product line maintains its own equivalent.)

### 4.4 `prov:Plan` and the transform recipe

`prov:Plan` (a subclass of `prov:Entity`) is *"a set of actions or steps intended by one or more agents to
achieve some goals"* ([w3.org/TR/prov-o](https://www.w3.org/TR/prov-o/)). For us it's the **named transform
recipe** an ingest run followed — the alias map, the unit scaling (cents→dollars, bps→decimal), the tz→UTC
normalization, the resample rule. Recording it as a `Plan` on the `qualifiedAssociation` (§3.1) means *"a
wrong number can be traced not just to a run but to the specific transform version that produced it,"*
which is the difference between "the data is wrong" and "transform v1.4.2's bps→percent step had an
off-by-100; here is every series it touched."

## 5. Putting license on the graph (the F2 defense, in PROV)

Pulling §2.2, §3, and §4.3 together, here is the complete licensing-on-PROV discipline — the model that
makes the red-team's **F2** goal (*"a composite that inherits a RED input yet claims GREEN"*) a thing CI
can catch:

1. **Verdict lives on the `Attribution` edge to the provider Organization** (§4.3). Never on the entity in
   isolation — that would let the same number carry two contradictory verdicts.
2. **`wasDerivedFrom` makes it transitive** (§2.2). The verdict for a derived entity is
   `AND` over the reachable ancestors' `commercialOk` (any RED ⇒ RED). This is a graph reachability query,
   runnable in CI.
3. **`hadPrimarySource` separates the publisher from the fetch path** (§2.2). FRED republishing a Treasury
   series: the *number's* primary source is Treasury (public-domain concept), but the *fetch path* is
   FRED, and FRED's redistribution ToS governs the verdict. Two different edges, two different roles, no
   confusion — exactly the "license attaches to the fetch path, not the concept" rule made structural.
4. **Failed/over-budget fetches don't get a fabricated verdict.** A run that returned `unavailable`/`needsKey`
   produces **no** output entity (or an entity flagged `prov:wasInvalidatedBy` the failed run) — never a
   backfilled value stamped GREEN to "look complete." (Non-negotiable #1 + the gate.)

> **The realest finding the negation loop can land here** (per `red-team-negation-loop.md` §G): the model
> is clean and the contamination query runs — but if a single composite is built **without** writing its
> `wasDerivedFrom` edges, the whole transitive guarantee silently fails for that series. So the capture
> recipe must make edge-writing **non-optional in the compose path**, not a thing you remember to do. PROV
> gives you the vocabulary; the pipeline discipline (`patterns-lineage-capture.md`) is what makes it true.

## 6. Building PROV programmatically — the Python `prov` library

The reference implementation for emitting real, serializable PROV from Python is **`prov`** (Trung Dong
Huynh, University of Southampton) — *"A Python library for W3C Provenance Data Model (PROV)"*
([github.com/trungdong/prov](https://github.com/trungdong/prov);
[prov.readthedocs.io](https://prov.readthedocs.io/)). It builds a `ProvDocument`, lets you assert all the
relations above, and serializes to **PROV-N**, **PROV-JSON**, **RDF (Turtle/etc.)**, and **PROV-XML**, plus
a Graphviz DOT visualization. This is what you reach for the day a consumer (or an auditor) asks for real
PROV — without it being your *storage* format (§7).

### 6.1 The full API, by example

Verbatim method shapes from the library docs ([prov.readthedocs.io usage](https://prov.readthedocs.io/en/latest/usage.html)),
assembled into our ingest example:

```python
import datetime
import prov.model as prov

# --- document + namespaces ---------------------------------------------------
d = prov.ProvDocument()
d.set_default_namespace("https://example.platform/series/")
d.add_namespace("prov", "http://www.w3.org/ns/prov#")
d.add_namespace("dct",  "http://purl.org/dc/terms/")
d.add_namespace("lic",  "https://example.platform/ns/license#")

# --- agents ------------------------------------------------------------------
treasury = d.agent("treasuryGov", {
    prov.PROV_TYPE: "prov:Organization",
    "dct:title": "U.S. Department of the Treasury",
})
pipeline = d.agent("ingestPipeline_v1_4_2", {
    prov.PROV_TYPE: "prov:SoftwareAgent",
    "dct:hasVersion": "1.4.2+sha.a1b2c3d",
})

# --- the input (raw upstream response) entity --------------------------------
raw = d.entity("treasuryGov_response_8f3c", {
    "dct:title": "treasury.gov daily-treasury-rates XML, 2026-06-24",
})

# --- the activity (the fetch+normalize+store run) ----------------------------
#     signature: activity(identifier, startTime=None, endTime=None, other_attributes=None)
run = d.activity(
    "ingestRun_8f3c",
    datetime.datetime(2026, 6, 24, 6, 9, 58),     # startedAtTime
    datetime.datetime(2026, 6, 24, 6, 10, 0),     # endedAtTime
    {prov.PROV_TYPE: "fetch-normalize-store"},
)

# --- the output entity (the normalized series) -------------------------------
series = d.entity("treasury_10y_2026-06-24", {
    "dct:title": "US 10Y Treasury constant-maturity yield",
})

# --- the relations -----------------------------------------------------------
d.used(run, raw, datetime.datetime(2026, 6, 24, 6, 9, 59))   # activity used entity
d.wasGeneratedBy(series, run,                                # entity <- activity
                 datetime.datetime(2026, 6, 24, 6, 10, 0))
d.wasAssociatedWith(run, pipeline)                           # activity <- agent
d.wasAttributedTo(series, treasury)                          # entity <- agent

# --- qualified attribution carrying the LICENSE (the F2 defense) -------------
#     emit the reified Attribution so commercialOk rides the edge:
d.attribution(series, treasury, other_attributes={
    "lic:commercialOk": True,
    "lic:basis": "17 USC 105 (US-gov public domain)",
    prov.PROV_ROLE: "lic:dataPublisher",
})

# --- a derived composite, with the contamination edge ------------------------
spread = d.entity("spread_ig_10y_2026-06-24")
corp   = d.entity("corp_ig_yield_2026-06-24")    # RED leg, elsewhere
d.wasDerivedFrom(spread, series)                 # GREEN ancestor
d.wasDerivedFrom(spread, corp)                   # RED ancestor -> contaminates

# --- revision (a re-fetch that corrects a prior series) ----------------------
series_v2 = d.entity("treasury_10y_2026-06-24__rev2")
d.wasDerivedFrom(series_v2, series,
                 other_attributes={prov.PROV_TYPE: "prov:Revision"})
```

Key API facts pinned from the docs:

- `ProvDocument()` — the container. `set_default_namespace(uri)` and `add_namespace(prefix, uri)` register
  prefixes ([usage docs](https://prov.readthedocs.io/en/latest/usage.html)).
- `d.entity(id, other_attributes=None)`, `d.activity(id, startTime=None, endTime=None, other_attributes=None)`,
  `d.agent(id, other_attributes=None)` — the three element constructors. Attributes are a dict or a tuple of
  `(key, value)` pairs; `prov.PROV_TYPE`, `prov.PROV_ROLE`, `prov.PROV_LABEL`, `prov.PROV_LOCATION` are the
  built-in qualified-name constants.
- Relation helpers mirror the PROV property names exactly: `wasGeneratedBy`, `used`, `wasAssociatedWith`,
  `wasAttributedTo`, `wasDerivedFrom`, `actedOnBehalfOf`, `wasInformedBy`, plus `attribution`, `association`,
  `usage`, `generation`, `derivation` for the qualified forms.
- Times are real `datetime.datetime` objects; the library renders them as `xsd:dateTime`.

### 6.2 Serialization — emit real PROV in any standard form

```python
# PROV-JSON (the default) ------------------------------------------------------
print(d.serialize(indent=2))               # -> PROV-JSON string

# PROV-N (the human-readable notation) -----------------------------------------
print(d.get_provn())
#   activity(ingestRun_8f3c, 2026-06-24T06:09:58, 2026-06-24T06:10:00, [prov:type="fetch-normalize-store"])
#   wasGeneratedBy(treasury_10y_2026-06-24, ingestRun_8f3c, 2026-06-24T06:10:00)
#   used(ingestRun_8f3c, treasuryGov_response_8f3c, 2026-06-24T06:09:59)
#   wasAttributedTo(treasury_10y_2026-06-24, treasuryGov)
#   ...

# RDF / Turtle (for the day a triplestore or DCAT consumer wants it) -----------
turtle = d.serialize(format="rdf", rdf_format="turtle")

# PROV-XML ---------------------------------------------------------------------
xml = d.serialize(format="xml")
```

Supported `serialize` format strings: `'json'` (default), `'rdf'` (with `rdf_format='turtle'|'xml'|'n3'|…`,
via `rdflib`), `'provn'`, `'xml'` ([prov.serializers package docs](https://prov.readthedocs.io/en/latest/prov.serializers.html)).
PROV-N PROV-N output matches the primer's notation exactly, e.g. the primer's
`wasGeneratedBy(exc:chart1, exc:compile1, 2012-03-02T10:30:00)`.

### 6.3 Visualization

```python
from prov.dot import prov_to_dot
dot = prov_to_dot(d)            # -> a pydot Dot
dot.write_png("ingest_lineage.png")
```

`prov_to_dot(bundle, show_nary=True, use_labels=False, direction='BT', show_element_attributes=True,
show_relation_attributes=True)` ([prov.dot docs](https://prov.readthedocs.io/en/latest/prov.html)) renders
the entity/activity/agent graph — useful for an ops dashboard or an audit artifact, not for the hot path.

> **Use the library at the edges, not the core.** Per the JPM theory's Pydantic-cost finding (per-row model
> construction balloons latency on bulk paths, line 125), do **not** build a `ProvDocument` per series on
> the ingest hot path — that is heavyweight RDF object construction. Build the provenance as plain
> dict/rows in the fetcher (cheap), persist those rows (§7), and **materialize a `ProvDocument` lazily** —
> only when an export/audit/DCAT consumer requests real PROV. The library is the *serializer at the
> boundary*, not the *in-memory representation on the write path*.

## 7. Why PROV-O is the vocabulary to borrow, not a mandate to ship a triplestore at v1

This is the scale judgment the whole doc hinges on, and it deserves to be stated as plainly as the JPM
theory states its read/write split.

**The trap.** A junior reading "use W3C PROV-O" concludes: *stand up Apache Jena / GraphDB / Blazegraph,
store every series's lineage as RDF triples, query with SPARQL.* For a system whose own theory targets
**130M+ time-series and 4B+ hits/year** ([00-theory.md](../../../../.agents/jpm-markets-reengineering/financial-data-analytics-service/00-theory.md),
line 13), that is the wrong call at v1, for the same reason its MAJOR-1 finding rejected pgvector as the
primary index (line 72): you'd be adopting a specialized engine for a property a relational store already
serves, and inheriting its scale ceiling and ops weight.

**The distinction that resolves it.** PROV-O is two separable things:

1. **A data model** — the classes, the relations, the edge directions, the semantics. *This is the part to
   adopt wholesale.* It costs nothing at runtime; it's a naming + schema discipline. Adopting it means our
   provenance rows have columns named and shaped after `wasGeneratedBy` / `wasDerivedFrom` /
   `wasAttributedTo`, and the contamination query is a recursive CTE over a `lineage_edge` table.

2. **A serialization + reasoning stack** — RDF triples, OWL inference, SPARQL, a triplestore. *This is
   optional and deferred.* You need it only when an external consumer wants interchange PROV or you want
   OWL reasoning. At v1 you don't; you have a known, finite set of edge types and a SQL store that already
   does reachability via recursive CTEs.

**The v1 implementation, therefore** (full recipe in `patterns-lineage-capture.md`):

- Provenance lives as **plain relational rows** in the same Postgres/TimescaleDB the JPM theory already
  selected (B2, line 150) — co-located with the catalog metadata, no new engine.
  - a `lineage_node` table (id, kind ∈ {entity, activity, agent}, type, attributes JSONB),
  - a `lineage_edge` table (src, dst, predicate ∈ {wasGeneratedBy, used, wasDerivedFrom, wasAttributedTo,
    wasAssociatedWith, …}, attributes JSONB),
  - or, denormalized for the common case, a `series_provenance` row per series carrying source-agent,
    fetch-path, fetched-at, run-id, commercialOk, basis, plus an edge list for derivations.
- The **contamination query** is a recursive CTE walking `wasDerivedFrom` edges and `AND`-ing
  `commercialOk` — indexed on `(src)` and `(dst)`, it's a bounded traversal (composites are shallow).
- **Real PROV (RDF/JSON-LD/PROV-N) is generated on demand** from those rows via the `prov` library (§6),
  at the export boundary only.

**Tier statement** (per `product-at-scale.md` discipline):

| Tier | Provenance load | What serves it | What breaks at the next tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1× (demo)** | 100s–1000s of series, no composites | a flat `commercialOk` field per series | no contamination check; composites can't be cleared |
| **100× (traction)** | 10k–100k series + composites | `series_provenance` rows + `lineage_edge` + recursive-CTE contamination query in Postgres | nothing structural; just index the edge columns |
| **10,000× (product)** | 1M+ series, deep composite DAGs, audit/interchange demand | same relational store; materialize a graph cache (or a real graph DB / triplestore) **only if** SPARQL interchange or deep-DAG traversal becomes a hot path | a triplestore is a *considered upgrade with a named trigger*, not a v1 default |

> **The senior call.** *Adopt the PROV-O model at v1 (it's free and it's the schema). Defer the PROV-O
> serialization/triplestore stack until a consumer or a traversal-depth forces it (named trigger, not
> reflex).* This is the same compute-once-serve-many / right-engine-for-the-query-shape discipline the
> data-analytics theory applies everywhere else — applied to provenance.

## 8. The bridge: PROV (the model) → OpenLineage (the operational run capture)

PROV describes *what the lineage IS* (the timeless graph: this entity derived from that, attributed to this
agent). **OpenLineage** describes *how to CAPTURE it operationally as pipelines run* — it's the run/job/dataset
event standard emitted by data pipelines, governed by **LF AI & Data**
([openlineage.io](https://openlineage.io/docs/spec/object-model);
[github.com/OpenLineage/OpenLineage](https://github.com/OpenLineage/OpenLineage)). The JPM theory pairs them
deliberately: *"W3C PROV … for the model + OpenLineage for run-level pipeline lineage"* (line 104, 120).

### 8.1 The model mapping (PROV ↔ OpenLineage)

The two vocabularies are isomorphic at the core — OpenLineage is, in effect, an operationally-shaped
profile of PROV:

| PROV-O | OpenLineage | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `prov:Activity` | **Job** (the process definition) + **Run** (one execution) | a process; OpenLineage splits the timeless *job* from the specific *run* (a finer cut than PROV's single Activity) |
| `prov:Entity` | **Dataset** | the data produced/consumed |
| `prov:used` | a Run's **inputs** | datasets consumed by the run |
| `prov:wasGeneratedBy` | a Run's **outputs** | datasets produced by the run |
| `prov:wasInformedBy` | input/output dataset chaining across runs | job-to-job dependency |
| `prov:startedAtTime` / `prov:endedAtTime` | START / COMPLETE **RunEvent** `eventTime`s | run boundaries |
| `prov:Agent` (`SoftwareAgent`) | the **producer** URI | who emitted the lineage |
| qualified attributes (`prov:hadRole`, schema, …) | **facets** (`schema`, `dataSource`, `columnLineage`, `dataQualityMetrics`, …) | the extensible annotation slot |

OpenLineage definitions, verbatim ([github.com/OpenLineage/OpenLineage `spec/OpenLineage.md`](https://github.com/OpenLineage/OpenLineage/blob/main/spec/OpenLineage.md)):
**Run** = *"an instance of a running job with a start and completion (or failure) time … identified by a
globally unique ID relative to its job definition."* **Job** = *"a process definition that consumes and
produces datasets."* **Dataset** = *"an abstract representation of data … a unique name within the
datasource namespace derived from its physical location."*

The decisive difference from PROV: OpenLineage **splits Activity into Job + Run.** PROV's `Activity` is one
execution; OpenLineage separately models the *recurring job* ("the daily treasury ingest") and each *run* of
it (the 2026-06-24 06:09 execution, `runId` UUID). For a system that ingests the **same series daily**, that
split is exactly right — it's the difference between "the job" and "today's run of the job," which PROV
would force you to model as N unrelated activities.

### 8.2 A RunEvent (verbatim) and how it maps

A START event ([OpenLineage spec](https://github.com/OpenLineage/OpenLineage/blob/main/spec/OpenLineage.md)):

```json
{
  "eventType": "START",
  "eventTime": "2020-12-09T23:37:31.081Z",
  "run":    { "runId": "3b452093-782c-4ef2-9c0c-aafe2aa6f34d" },
  "job":    { "namespace": "my-scheduler-namespace", "name": "myjob.mytask" },
  "inputs":  [ { "namespace": "my-datasource-namespace", "name": "instance.schema.table" } ],
  "outputs": [ { "namespace": "my-datasource-namespace", "name": "instance.schema.output_table" } ],
  "producer": "https://github.com/OpenLineage/OpenLineage/blob/v1-0-0/client",
  "schemaURL": "https://openlineage.io/spec/1-0-0/OpenLineage.json#/definitions/RunEvent"
}
```

Mapped to our ingest:

```json
{
  "eventType": "COMPLETE",
  "eventTime": "2026-06-24T06:10:00Z",
  "run":  { "runId": "8f3c...uuid",
            "facets": { "nominalTime": { "nominalStartTime": "2026-06-24T06:00:00Z" } } },
  "job":  { "namespace": "platform.ingest", "name": "treasury.daily_10y" },
  "inputs":  [ { "namespace": "treasury.gov", "name": "daily-treasury-rates" } ],
  "outputs": [ { "namespace": "platform.series",
                 "name": "treasury_10y",
                 "facets": {
                   "schema":     { "fields": [ { "name": "date", "type": "DATE" },
                                               { "name": "yield", "type": "DECIMAL" } ] },
                   "dataSource": { "name": "treasury.gov", "uri": "https://home.treasury.gov/..." }
                 } } ],
  "producer": "https://example.platform/ingest@v1.4.2",
  "schemaURL": "https://openlineage.io/spec/2-0-2/OpenLineage.json#/definitions/RunEvent"
}
```

Contract rules pinned from the spec: *exactly one `START` and one of `[COMPLETE, ABORT, FAIL]` per run*;
`eventType` ∈ {`START`, `RUNNING`, `COMPLETE`, `ABORT`, `FAIL`}; `eventTime` is ISO-8601;
`producer` is *"a URI identifying the producer of this metadata … could be a git url with a given tag or
sha"*; `schemaURL` is *"an immutable pointer to the version of the … schema."* **Facets** are *"an atomic
piece of metadata attached to a Job, Run, or Dataset … modular and extensible"* — dataset facets include
`schema`, `dataSource`, `version`, `columnLineage`, `dataQualityMetrics`; run facets include `nominalTime`,
`parent`, `errorMessage`, `sql`; job facets include `sourceCode`, `sourceCodeLocation`
([github.com/OpenLineage/OpenLineage `spec/OpenLineage.md`](https://github.com/OpenLineage/OpenLineage/blob/main/spec/OpenLineage.md);
[openlineage.io object-model](https://openlineage.io/docs/spec/object-model)).

### 8.3 Which to use where (the design call)

- **PROV-O is our *resting* model** — the timeless lineage graph we store and query: what derived from
  what, what's attributed to whom, what's licensed how. It's where the **contamination query** and the
  **license verdict** live. Storage = relational rows shaped after PROV (§7); export = real PROV via the
  `prov` library (§6).
- **OpenLineage is our *capture* protocol** — the event each ingest run *emits as it executes* (START at
  fetch begin, COMPLETE/FAIL at store/abort), carrying the run/job/dataset identity and facets. A pipeline
  integration (an OpenLineage client in the worker) emits these; a backend (Marquez, or our own consumer)
  ingests them and **projects them into the PROV resting model.** The `commercialOk` verdict travels as a
  **custom dataset facet** on the output dataset, then lands on the `Attribution` edge in PROV.
- **The flow:** *run emits OpenLineage events → consumer projects Run→Activity, Dataset→Entity,
  producer→Agent, inputs/outputs→used/wasGeneratedBy, custom-license-facet→qualifiedAttribution → stored as
  PROV-shaped rows → contamination/verdict queried in SQL → real PROV serialized on demand.*

> **Net for the build.** Don't choose one *or* the other — they're different layers. OpenLineage is the
> wire format for *capturing* lineage as pipelines run (it knows about jobs, runs, facets, and has client
> libraries); PROV is the *semantic model* for *reasoning about* lineage and binding our license verdict.
> Capture with OpenLineage, reason/store/serve with PROV. The recipe that wires both is
> `patterns-lineage-capture.md`.

## 9. Anti-patterns (mistake → fix)

| Anti-pattern | Why it's wrong | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| A flat `{"source": "..."}` string instead of a `wasAttributedTo` edge to an Agent | can't express derivation → can't catch contamination (F2); can't distinguish publisher from fetch-path | Agent is a node; `wasAttributedTo` is an edge; license rides the qualified `Attribution`. |
| Reversing edge direction (`activity wasGeneratedBy entity`) | inverts the meaning; breaks every PROV consumer and the contamination traversal | `wasGeneratedBy`: **Entity → Activity**. `used`: **Activity → Entity**. They're opposite siblings (§2.1). |
| Putting `commercialOk` on the `prov:Entity` directly | the same number has different licenses by fetch path; an entity-level verdict can't represent that | verdict lives on the **`prov:Attribution`** edge to the provider **Organization** (§4.3). |
| Building a composite without writing `wasDerivedFrom` edges | the transitive contamination guarantee silently fails for that series (the realest negation finding, §5) | edge-writing is **non-optional in the compose path**, enforced by the pipeline, not remembered. |
| Standing up a triplestore / SPARQL stack at v1 | adopts a specialized engine for a property relational rows + a recursive CTE already serve; same error as the pgvector-as-primary-index rejection | adopt the **model**; store PROV-shaped **rows**; serialize real PROV on demand; triplestore only on a named trigger (§7). |
| Building a `ProvDocument` per series on the ingest hot path | heavyweight RDF object construction per row balloons latency (the Pydantic-cost pathology) | build cheap dict/rows on the write path; materialize `ProvDocument` **lazily** at the export boundary only (§6.3). |
| Treating OpenLineage and PROV as competitors / picking one | they're different layers — capture-protocol vs resting-model | capture with OpenLineage, reason/store/serve with PROV; project one into the other (§8.3). |
| Mutating a series in place on a re-fetch | violates entity immutability; loses point-in-time/as-of history | a revision is a **new entity** `wasRevisionOf` the old (§2.2) — the store stays append-only. |
| Backfilling a failed fetch with a value and stamping it GREEN | violates "never invent a finance number" + the gate | a failed run yields **no** output entity (or `wasInvalidatedBy`); return typed `unavailable`/`needsKey` (§5). |
| Confusing DCAT's `dcat:DatasetSeries` with our financial "series" | DCAT v3's `DatasetSeries` = an *ordered collection of datasets*, a different sense | map our series to a Dataset-or-finer granularity; disambiguate in the model (per 00-theory MINOR, line 103). |

## 10. Output contract — what "done" looks like for a provenance stamp

A series's provenance record is **PROV-correct** when it satisfies all of:

1. **Three classes present.** The series is a typed `prov:Entity`; the fetch+normalize run is a typed
   `prov:Activity`; the provider is a typed `prov:Agent`/`prov:Organization`; our pipeline is a
   `prov:SoftwareAgent`.
2. **Core edges asserted, correct direction.** `series wasGeneratedBy run`; `run used <raw-input>`;
   `series wasAttributedTo provider`; `run wasAssociatedWith pipeline`. (§2.1 directions exactly.)
3. **Times stamped.** `run` has `startedAtTime`/`endedAtTime`; `series` has `generatedAtTime` (= our
   `fetchedAt`).
4. **License on the attribution edge.** A `prov:qualifiedAttribution` → `prov:Attribution` carries
   `commercialOk` + `basis` + a ledger-row link, bound to the provider Organization — **not** on the entity
   in isolation. (§4.3 / §5.)
5. **Derivation edges for every composite.** Each input series of a composite has a `wasDerivedFrom` edge
   from the composite to it; the composite's verdict is the `AND` over reachable ancestors. (§2.2 / §5.)
6. **Revisions are new entities.** A re-fetch/correction produces a new entity `wasRevisionOf` the prior —
   the store stays append-only. (§2.2.)
7. **Transform is named.** The activity carries a `prov:hadPlan` (or a transform-version attribute)
   identifying the exact normalization recipe applied. (§4.4.)
8. **Serializable to real PROV.** The record can be rendered to PROV-N / PROV-JSON / RDF on demand via the
   `prov` library — proving the model (not just a bespoke schema) was followed. (§6.)
9. **Tiered honestly.** The implementation states which tier it survives (flat field / relational graph /
   triplestore) and what triggers the next-tier move — no triplestore shipped while a recursive CTE
   suffices. (§7.)

A record missing #4 or #5 is the **F2 hole** the negation loop is built to land — a composite that *could*
inherit RED but has no edge to prove it clean. A record missing #2's directions is broken for every PROV
consumer. Those two are the load-bearing ones.
